# GYLDEN'S THEORY

# 10.18GYLDEN's INTERMEDIARY ORBIT

The usual procedure in the method of the variation of elements is to take a Keplerian ellipse as a first approximation and to study the deviation from this Keplerian motion. Gyldén's new approach was to adopt an orbit different from a Keplerian ellipse as his first approximation.Such an orbit is called an intermediary orbit, (Gyldén, l88l, 1882). He studied the intermediary orbits of a comet (l877,1884) and of the moon (l885).Gyldén amalgamated,in the intermediary orbit,the secular and the long-period terms in the perturbation of the Keplerian motion and called such an orbit an absolute orbit.He expressed the motion in such an intermediary orbit in a purely trigonometrical series which he mistakenly thought to be convergent. The deviations from such an intermediary orbit turned out to be purely periodic fluctuations.However,even for the intermediary orbit, the solution was very complicated. He made use of elliptic functions.His works are summarized in two volumes published in 1893 and 1909.

A space curve contained between two concentric spheres and with its concave side facing always toward the common center of the two spheres is said to be periplegmatic. The radius vector of a periplegmatic curve is of length between the radii of the two spheres.At the maxima and minima of the radius vector the curve touches respectively the larger or smaller of the two limiting spheres.The radial distance between such a maximum and minimum is called the diastem. The general equation of a periplegmatic curve is represented by

$$
y \frac { d ^ { 2 } y } { d x ^ { 2 } } = - \Pi , \qquad \Pi > 0 .
$$

Gyldén tried to solve the differential equations for a planetary motion by means of purely trigonometric functions with linear functions of $t$ as arguments.A term in his theory which corresponds to a periodic inequality in ordinary pertrubation theory is called a coordinate term. A term which does not vanish even when the disturbing mass is put equal to zero and which reduces to a term in an elliptic motion of the disturbed planet around the sun is called an elementary term.This idea was reviewed by Wolf (1891)，Backlund (1889)，and Olsson (l891).In Gyldén's process of integration,terms appear with the disturbing mass in the denominator,that is, terms which become infinite when the disturbing mass vanishes, although they appear only in the process of integration but not in the final result. Such terms are called hyper-elementary. Terms of long period arising from the presence of the so-called small divisors are called characteristic, sometimes semi-elementary.

Another aspect of Gyldén's theory is the use of the true anomaly as the independent variable in place of time, where Hansen and Newcomb used the eccentric anomaly. Then the series converge more rapidly, although only in a formal sense.Gyldén obtained the solution in terms of purely trigonometric series by referring to the works of Hermite and Lamé on elliptic functions.Gyldén thought such series as he employed to be convergent.He called his method of solving the nonlinear differential equation the heuristic method.However,Poincaré proved that Gyldén's series are not uniformly convergent (Chapter l5).
