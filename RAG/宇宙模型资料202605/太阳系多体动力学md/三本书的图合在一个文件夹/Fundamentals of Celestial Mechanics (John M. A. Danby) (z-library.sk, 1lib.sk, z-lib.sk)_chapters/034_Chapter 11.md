57.Musen,P.Modified Formulae for Hansen's Special Perturbations.Astron. J.,63,426-429,1958.

The planetary equations are used to solve, to the first order, the problem of the motion of an artificial satellite in the field of an oblate planet in:

58.Kozai,Y.The Motion of a Close Earth Satellite.Astron.J.,64,367-377, 1959.

Herrick's method for the variation of parameters is given in:

59.Herrick,S.A Modification of the “Variation of Constants”Method for Special Perturbations.P.A.S.P.60,321-323,1948.

60.Herrick, S. Icarus and the Variation of Parameters.Astron.J., 58,156- 164,1953.

Herrick gives a discussion of the integral for M in:

61.Herrick, S. The Mean Longitude or Mean Anomaly in Perturbations by Variation of Constants. Astron.J., 56,186-188,1951.

Merton's method is given in:

62.Merton,G.A Modification of the Perturbation-of-Elements Method. M.N.R.A.S.,109,421-435,1949.

Poincare's method was first applied in:

63.Henon,M.and Heiles,C. The Application of the Third Integral:Some Numerical Experiments.Astron.J.,60,73-79,1964.

Some applications to astronomical topics can be found in:

64.Danby,J.M.A.High-Energy Orbitsin Barred Spirals.Astron.J.,72,919- 925,1967.
