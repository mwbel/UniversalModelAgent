# 6.2.1 Pulsars as stable clocks

Neutron stars are rapidly spinning,with rotational periods as small as $1 . 5 \mathrm { m s }$ This is a consequence of the conservation of angular momentum during the collapse, since $\omega r ^ { 2 }$ stays constant while $r$ decreases from the typical stellar size of the original star core down to a radius of just $1 0 ~ \mathrm { k m }$ It is thought that the supernova collapse can spin them up to maybe 1O ms. The magnetic field is in general misaligned with the rotation axis,so it has the structure of a rotating dipole,as in Fig. 6.2. If $\rho$ denotes the distance from the rotation axis in cylindrical coordinates,there is a critical distance $\rho _ { c } = c / \Omega$ (where $\Omega$ is the angular velocity of the pulsar)such that the magnetic field lines that reach a distance $\rho > \rho _ { c }$ are open and escape to infnity，while those contained within $\rho _ { c }$ are closed.
