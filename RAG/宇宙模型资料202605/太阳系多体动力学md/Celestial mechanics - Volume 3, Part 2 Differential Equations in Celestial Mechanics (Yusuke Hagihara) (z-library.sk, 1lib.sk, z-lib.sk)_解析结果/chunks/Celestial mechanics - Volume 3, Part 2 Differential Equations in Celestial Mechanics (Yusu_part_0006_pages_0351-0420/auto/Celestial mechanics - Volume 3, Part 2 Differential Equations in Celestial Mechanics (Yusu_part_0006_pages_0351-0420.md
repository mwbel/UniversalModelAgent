Poincaré(1893) extended the study to clarifying the mechanism of divergence of Bohlin's series (Section 14.12), that is,what term of the series causes the increase of the sum so that the series is no longer convergent.

# 16.1DEFINITION OF STABILITY

In the theory of differential equations there is an endeavor to obtain a definitive solution or one of definitive type,either in closed form, which is rarely possible, or else by some process of approximation; this latter approach has been the main topic of the previous chapters. On the other hand, one seeks to obtain information about the whole class of solutions instead of reaching either exact or approximate solutions. This latter area of study was initiated by Poincaré (1885, 1879,1899).A major problem in such qualitative theory is the relation of a solution to its neighbors.A solution in general represents a curve or a trajectory $C$ .The question is whether the trajectories $C ^ { \prime }$ starting near $C$ are recurrent or periodic,in particular,whether they tend to remain in the immediate neighborhood $B$ of $C$ ，in which case $C$ is stable,or depart from $\pmb { B }$ ， in which case $C$ is unstable,or whether $C ^ { \prime }$ fills up a finite restricted domain everywhere densely,in which case it is said to be stable in Poisson's sense or ergodic. The topological theory that will be treated later in Chapters 22 to 27 is of such a qualitative character. The notion of stability appears in all such qualitative theories (Routh，1877,1884,1905).Liapounov(1907, 1949,1966) in 1892 consolidated the theory of stability by refining Poincaré's idea on characteristic exponents (Section 19.1).Liapounov called the first method the derivation of an explicit solution known in definite series form which is only applicable to some restricted but important cases.Liapounov's second method, called direct, does not require the knowledge of the solution itself；instead,it demands to formulate the so-called Liapounov function or the Liapounov functional (Section 15.10) for the criterion of stability (Malkin,1952;Bellman, 1953；Hurewicz,1958；Hahn，1959；Cesari,1959；La Salle and Lefschetz，1961；Cetaev，1961；Hale，1963；Krasovskii，1963; Zubov,1964； Coppel,1965； Shtokalo,1961; Liu and Leake,1967; Pliss,1966； Roseau, 1966；Bhatia and Szego,1970).
