Meanplace:typically whatis found inmost starcatalogs.Itis the barycentric directionofanon-solar system object that takes into account its orbital and space motions (as applicable). Conceptually,it isthedirectionoftheobjectas itwouldbeobserved fromthe solarsystembarycenterata specified date,with respect to specified coordinate axes (usually the mean equator and equinox of a specified date orthe ICRS),if the masses of the Sun and other solar system bodies were negligible.Do not confuse thiswith a“mean”reference system.

Geometric place:typically theoutput of solar systemephemerides.Itis theposition ofasolar system object defined by a straight line (vector) between the origin (the solar system barycenter unless otherwise stated) and the object at agiven time,without any corrections forlight-time,aberration, light deflection, etc.

Properplace:the geocentric direction of an object that takes into account orbital motion,space motion,and light-time (asapplicable),light deflection,and annual aberration.Unless otherwise stated,it is expressed withrespect to theGCRSaxes.Conceptually,itis the directionof theobject asit would be observed from the center of the Earth if the Earth were transparent,non-refracting, and massless.

Apparent place: the proper place of anobject expressed with respect to the true equator and equinox of date.

Intermediate place: the proper place of an object expressed with respect to the true equator and CIO of date.

Astrometric place:the geocentric direction of asolar system objectcomputed by applying the correctionfor light-time displacement to the geometric position.Such a position is directly comparable withthecatalogpositions of background stars in the same areaof the sky,after the star positions have been updated for proper motion and parallax.An astrometric place is expressed with respect to the axes of the reference system of a star catalog,e.g.,the ICRS axes.

Topocentric place:the proper place of an object transformed to a specific location on or near the surface of the Earth.Atmospheric refraction is ignored.It is expressed with respect to the true equatorand either the true equinox ofdate or CIO of date.Itis similar toan apparentor intermediate place,but includescorrections for geocentricparallaxand diurnal aberration.

Local place: a topocentric place of an object expressed with respect to the GCRS axes.
