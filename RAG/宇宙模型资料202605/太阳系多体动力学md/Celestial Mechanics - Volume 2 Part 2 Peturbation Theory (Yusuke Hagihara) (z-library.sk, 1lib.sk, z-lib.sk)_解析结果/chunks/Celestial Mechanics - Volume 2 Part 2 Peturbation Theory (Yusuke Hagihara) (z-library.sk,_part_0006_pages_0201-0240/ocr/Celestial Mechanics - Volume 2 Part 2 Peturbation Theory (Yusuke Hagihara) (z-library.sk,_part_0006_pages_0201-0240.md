Breakwell and Perko (1966) matched heliocentric ellipses - corrected for the influence of planetary perturbation in the neighborhood of a planet - with local hyperbolas. They found that the attraction of the destination planet alone causes a displacement of the arrival asymptote and a correction for the time of arrival. This last correction consists of two parts: a gross time bias and a local time bias, the latter depending only on the eccentricity of the arrival hyperbola. Similar corrections, including velocity corrections, are needed by the effect of the planet of departure. The method was applied for the computation of flyby interplanetary trajectories such as a trajectory from the earth to Mars via Venus,and the computation was seen to be greatly simplified if the planetary attractions could be neglected and the trajectory idealized as two heliocentric Keplerian orbits joined together.

Breuer and Riddell (l964) analyzed the minimum inclination of the lunicentered orbital plane of an earth-launched vehicle to the moon's equatorial plane. The general characteristics of the launch window, e.g., the allowable azimuth angle for orbital launch to the moon,were presented by Reich (l964).He gave the earth-orbit launch criteria for lunar trajectories, the departure trajectory sensitivities to orbital parameters,the mission and vehicle sensitivities to time and energy requirements,and the analytical technique for determining optimum launch conditions. The launch windows were further studied by Shute (1964).

The characteristics of trajectories that initiate on the moon's surface and terminate at a specific landing site on the earth's surface were studied by Dallas (l964).He employed the matched conic method of Egorov and Penzo.Penzo (l964） himself analyzed the free return of circumlunar trajectories with a simplified model of motion in the earth-moon system. Penzo's model was based on the concept of the sphere of activity,inside which the trajectory is represented by a mooninfluenced conic and outside which it is represented by an earthinfluenced conic.The conics are so chosen that the position and velocity match at the boundary of the sphere.Penzo classified six possible circumlunar trajectories. Johnson (l964) treated a similar problem for free-return circumlunar trajectories from launch windows with fixed launch azimuths.

Shute (l966)， for studying moon-to-earth trajectories,employed a reduced form，obtained by considering the selenocentric velocity asymptotes,of the matched-conic method for determining the initial elements of a particle launched or ejected from the moon's surface with any arbitrary starting conditions.He derived explicit analytic functions for geocentric energy, the Jacobi constant,angular momentum, standard orbital elements,and the conditions for moon-to-earth trajectories.
